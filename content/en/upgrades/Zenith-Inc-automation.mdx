<Section icon={Settings} title="Core Requirements for the ERP System">

To achieve successful **Zenith Inc automation**, the proposed ERP system must meet a rigorous set of functional requirements. These requirements are divided into "Must-Have" features, which are essential for day-one operations, and "Nice-to-Have" features, which provide additional value and "flavor" to the system. One of the most critical technical capabilities is the system's ability to automatically detect and differentiate between purchase orders, product delivery notes, and invoices.

Furthermore, the system must support a global workforce. This includes a robust multi-language interface to accommodate the company's diverse staff and international partners. The automation of the vendor registration process, including the generation of unique IDs and the establishment of an organizational hierarchy for authorizations, is a cornerstone of this digital transformation.

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Requirement">
The system must automatically detect document types (PO, Delivery Note, Invoice) to enhance integration capabilities and support automated reconciliation.
</Callout>

| Feature Category | Requirement | Priority |

| **Vendor Management** | Online registration and unique ID generation | High (Must-Have) |
| **Security** | Role-based authorization hierarchy | High (Must-Have) |
| **Localization** | Support for Chinese and Spanish (Mexican) | High (Must-Have) |
| **Document Processing** | Automatic PO and Invoice detection | High (Must-Have) |
| **Workflow Tracking** | Percentage-based job completion status | Medium (Nice-to-Have) |

<Section icon={Target} title="The Automated Workflow Process">

The user journey within the new automated system is designed to be as intuitive as modern retail platforms like eBay. From the initial order placement to the final delivery notification, every step is tracked and validated by the ERP. This workflow ensures that customer data is handled securely and that all transactions are authorized through a multi-step authentication process.

The automation starts at the application phase, where customers fill out item quantities and locations. Once finalized, the system triggers a credit card authorization process followed by a secure login authentication to verify the user's identity.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Order Finalization">
    Customers select items, quantities, and delivery locations through the ERP interface.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Payment Authorization">
    The system requests credit card authorization to ensure funds are available for the transaction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="User Authentication">
    The user provides a username and password to authenticate the login for secure processing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Payment Processing">
    Customers select their preferred payment method and provide final billing details.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Notification">
    Automated email or phone notifications are sent to the customer once the package is in the delivery phase.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="User Experience">
The goal of this workflow is to mirror the simplicity of top-tier e-commerce sites while maintaining the robust security of an enterprise-grade ERP.
</Callout>

</Section>
